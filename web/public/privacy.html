Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 20, 2026

BikeRouteNicePDX ("the Service," "we," "us") is a free, personal-project bicycle-routing app for the Portland, Oregon area. We built it to be privacy-respecting: there are no user accounts, no advertising, and no analytics or third-party tracking. This policy explains what limited data the Service handles and why.

The short version
  • No account, no sign-in, no profile.
  • We don't run ads, analytics, or tracking pixels.
  • Your location is used to draw routes and is not stored by us.
  • Address searches and routing requests are sent to data providers only to fulfill your request.
  • Small preferences (like "tour seen") live in your browser, not on a server.

1. Location Data

If you tap "use my location," your browser or device asks your permission and provides your current GPS coordinates to the app. We use those coordinates only to set your starting point and display your position on the map. Your location is processed on your device and, when you request a route, sent to our routing server solely to compute that route. We do not store your location, build a location history, or associate it with your identity. You can decline or revoke location permission at any time in your browser or device settings; the app still works if you type addresses manually.

2. Addresses and Route Requests

When you search for an address or place, that text is sent to a third-party geocoding service (OpenStreetMap's Nominatim) to convert it into map coordinates. When you request a route, the start and end coordinates are sent to our routing server (and underlying open-source routing engines) to calculate directions. These requests are used only to return your result. We do not retain a personal profile from them. Standard web-server logs (such as IP address and request time) may be generated transiently for security and reliability and are not used to identify or track you.

3. Local Storage on Your Device

The Service stores a small amount of data in your browser's local storage — for example, a flag indicating you've already seen the introductory tour — so the app behaves sensibly on repeat visits. This data stays on your device, is not transmitted to us, and can be cleared at any time by clearing your browser's site data.

4. Map Tiles and Third-Party Data

Maps and routing rely on third-party resources, including OpenStreetMap (© OpenStreetMap contributors, ODbL), the OpenFreeMap basemap, the Nominatim geocoder, and Portland Bureau of Transportation (PBOT) open data. Loading map tiles and geocoding results involves requests to these providers, which receive standard request information (such as your IP address) as a normal part of serving content. Their handling of that data is governed by their own privacy policies.

5. No Sale of Data

We do not sell, rent, or trade any data about you. We have no advertising partners and run no behavioral tracking.

6. Children's Privacy

The Service is not directed to children under 13 and does not knowingly collect personal information from them.

7. Security

We take reasonable measures to operate the Service securely, but no method of transmission or storage is completely secure. Because the Service does not maintain accounts or store personal profiles, there is very little personal data at risk.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date above reflects the most recent revision. Continued use of the Service after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
